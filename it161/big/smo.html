Social Media Optimization

Social Media has an increasingly growing impact on our lives. Companies understandably want to take advantage of social media and its potential to help grow their business. Social media optimization or SMO is the practice of managing and controlling a social media campaign/plan to maximize awareness and growth, and to drive more traffic to a website. Most well-known companies (such as Amazon or Nordstrom’s) utilize SMO strategies for their site.

The term social media optimization was originally coined back in 2006 in an article by author and marketing strategist Rohit Bhargava. He outlined five rules that he has since updated after Twitter and other similar platforms became popular. Some of Rohit’s SMO rules focus on creating quality content that people actually want to see and share that they don’t consider to be spam. He also suggests making what you are posting easy for people to share across social media platforms. It’s possible to do this using quick sharing and bookmarking options that are readily available. Companies can offer contests with rewards on social media to users who leave a comment on their page. One of the key rules Rohit discusses is allowing other users to take and remix content to make it their own. The implementation of these various tactics have changed the way users experience social media.

SMO tools can help you share your content or posts faster and with less hassle by linking all your social medias together. Some media managements sites like Loomly, Hootsuite and Sprout Social offer easy to use calendars and figure out the optimum time of day to share your post depending on your businesses factors. They also can simultaneously send out all your post or spread them out and post them at specific times. This ensures that the target audience is exposed to your content. Companies that stick to SMO strategies have a better chance at success.
