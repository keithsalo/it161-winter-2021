Web Forms

Web forms are ubiquitous over the internet. They can be anything online that you fill out with information. Ranging from filling out a suggestion box online for a company, entering a contest, or subscribing to an email newsletter. Web forms can also entail putting your payment information on a site. Web forms are a crucial part for online commerce. Companies can learn additional information and target potential customers more effectively. By seeing customers buying habits through past purchases.

When you are building a web form for a page you start by creating the structure and values you want your form to have in HTML. Then with CSS you can improve your form’s look by changing the format from a basic default to have a more aesthetically pleasing design.

There are also web form building programs like Hubspot and Gravity Forms” that simplify the process of designing a form. They offer different templets and a variety of input form field options. These forms can be as basic or elaborate as you want.

Some of the best practices to utilize when designing a web form include:

  • Keep it simple and straightforward, avoid using unnecessary questions and only ask for relevant data. Users should know exactly what type of form this is and be able to understand it quickly.
  • Include brief understandable instructions that users can find and access. This will better ensure that form is filled out correctly.
  • Make action buttons such as “submit” or “pay” specific and stand out so that the user is clear on what will occur when it is clicked. Make these buttons stand out so they are easier to find.
  • Keep your form organized in a linear fashion. When you use multiple columns sometimes the input fields can get skipped or misinterpreted. A linear form flows better and allows the user to intuitively know what to input next.

Most of us find filling out forms tedious so anything we can do as designers to make the process more straightforward and efficient will be beneficial to both the user and the web client.
